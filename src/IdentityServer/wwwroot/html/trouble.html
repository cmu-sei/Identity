Things We Can Change

It is possible that we failed to include your issuer's certificate in our bundle of accepted issuers. Please send your certificate's issuer common name.

It is also possible that we do not support some older certificates with weaker cryptography. Usually, this just affects certificates that are signed with the sha1RSA algorithm.

Things You Can Change

Start with a fresh browser. Browsers tend to vary in how they maintain the state of your certificates. An easy test is to close all your browser windows before opening one to try again. You can try opening a private or incognito browser also, but if that doesn't work, you should still try the "close-all-first" approach.

Be sure to select the correct certificate when the browser prompts you. Some access card, like USGov PIV cards, present two certificates, only one of which is valid for client authentication. (Usually the first one displayed in the list.)

It is possible your web proxy is mangling the TLS handshake and thus not providing your client certificate to the web server. If you can authenticate to other sites using your certificate, it is worth asking your admins if this site needs a bypass from your proxy.

If you are behind an enterprise web proxy, there is not much troubleshooting with which we can help. We'd most likely need a packet capture of your TLS handshake from outside your web proxy. And that will probably just show us that your client certificate is not being included in the handshake.
